Comparison



Parameters Open Differentials Locker Differential Limited Slip Differential
Utility Mostly, the cars are equipped with the Open Differential because they are designed for running on the dry patches and city roads which are generally smooth and doesn’t require any extra efforts from the machine. Vehicles such as tow trucks, forklifts, tractors, and heavy equipment use locking differentials to maintain traction, especially when driving on soft, muddy, or uneven surfaces. Lockers are common in agricultural equipment and military trucks. On some farm tractors, there is a pedal that can be stepped on with the operator's heel to lock the differential as needed. The Limited Slip Differentials offers lifted traction in all weather conditions; be it loose gravel or the buttery snow.
Advantages Open Differential allows different wheel speeds, which won’t let wheel spin when going around a corner. A locked differential forces both left and right wheels on the same axle to rotate at the same speed under nearly all circumstances, without regard to tractional differences seen at either wheel. Therefore, each wheel can apply as much rotational force as the traction under it will allow, and the torques on each side-shaft will be unequal. A limited-slip differential prevents excessive power from being allocated to one wheel, and so keeps both wheels in powered rotation, ensuring that the traction will not be limited to the wheel which can handle the minimum amount of power.
Disadvantages If one tire is on a slippery surface, the supplied torque will easily overcome the available traction at a very low number the tire on the slippery surface will simply spin, absorbing all of the actual power output. Because they do not operate as smoothly as standard differentials, automatic locking differentials are often responsible for increased tire wear. Some older automatic locking differentials are known for making a clicking or banging noise when locking and unlocking as the vehicle negotiates turns. A disadvantage with a limited-slip differential is when you try to get started on an icy road from a stopped position. Usually with a limited-slip you have one wheel that is exerting most of the power. If this wheel looses traction on the ice then your car will slip and fish-tail.
Complexity Open differentials are the simplest Locking differentials are more complex than open differentials but simpler than limited slip differentials Limited Slip Differentials are the most complex ones as they contain clutch mechanism for functioning
Maintenance These are easy to maintain These are slightly difficult to maintain as they may wear out if used wrongly These require very high maintenance as the system is complex and the clutch/pressure plates tend to wear out, reducing power output
Efficiency Open differentials are efficient as long as both the wheels have required traction These differentials are efficient as long as they are engaged in straight line motion These differentials are highly efficient and hence used in racing cars but need to be maintained for high efficiency.
